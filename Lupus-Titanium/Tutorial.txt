1 - Click 'Filter requests: ...' to enable / edit request filtering.

2 - Click 'Capture requests'.

3 - Open a web browser and do typical actions
     (e.g. logging in, browsing a page) at your awesome web application of choice.

	 All captured requests can be grouped by clicking 'Add User Action'.
     You can view grouped and ungrouped requests in the lower pane.
	 It is also possible to remove user actions and change their names and to
	 inspect and remove requests.

	 You can search requests for text contained in urls, headers and bodies by 
	 clicking 'Find requests'.

4 - Click 'Stop capturing'.

5 - Get the requests through a tcp socket (localhost:9990) sending 'flat' or 'json' to it (whithout the quotes).
